It is primarily used to improve post-harvest ( stacked ), pre-processed images.

BASIC iDEA

Most of us make our recordings of light-polluted places, perhaps not with the most profitable cameras. This can see the quality of our crude images as long as they have been burdened with a high noise caused by light pollution and lack of cooling.

The Levon1 routine (classic) simply creates an RGB statistic from the image. On this basis, it uses a differentiated deduction in each of the color channels and then retrieves it to increase the useful noise level threshold.
See the illustrations below:

1 . Figure: Crude and Levon rutin corrected M 13 image

2 . Figure: Rosette fog before and after routine Levon1 routine

Originally, I just wanted to include this effective deduction routine in the program, but the demands increased. The routine is much more flexible if you can parameterize it. Indeed, in some cases it is advantageous to use a value other than the multiplier of RGB channels.

3 . Figure: North America Fog: After Levon1 and 2 routine

The Levon2 - Balanced deduction method can be applied to low-contrast faint objects. Here the intensity remaining after deduction is selectively amplified: the weaker regions are better than the bright ones. Thus, the galaxy seeds do not burn.

WHAT IT DO NOT KNOWS?

Of course, any emerging image repair trick is not elaborate, but there is an increasing scope for opportunities.
However, I do not want to sacrifice the main benefits of the program, simplicity and ease of handling.

Any digital image enhancement can only produce and amplify the information that was in it.

Mathematical methods, fortunately, provide very effective picture retention possibilities, leaving a lot of room for creative imagination.

- It does not know what to do with the professionally processed images. it just hurts them.
My program is a "miracle" with light-polluted, noisy, backlit images, which astrophysicists can truly appreciate.

 "It does not work with pale, witty, veiled fogs. In many cases, the LEVON-ó routine simply eliminates these fine structures. Maybe the Levon2 routine is more advantageous in this case.

 - Many people complain that the Levon routine is so much that it cuts the background to make it black. Too sterile background! they say. It's a taste of tasting, although I'm getting more and more interested in it.

I am constantly working on solving problems.
